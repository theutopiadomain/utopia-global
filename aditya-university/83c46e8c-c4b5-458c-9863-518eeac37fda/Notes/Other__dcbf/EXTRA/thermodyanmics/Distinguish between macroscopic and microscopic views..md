## Macroscopic and Microscopic Viewpoints

| Feature                          | ==Microscopic Approach==                                                      | ==Macroscopic Approach==                                                   |
| -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Also Known As**                | _Statistical_ Approach                                                        | _Classical_ Approach                                                       |
| **Focus**                        | Behavior of _individual_ molecules or _particles_                             | System _considered_ as a _whole_, without focusing on individual molecules |
| **Properties Based On**          | _Molecular motion_ and _interactions_                                         | _Average_ or bulk _properties of matter_                                   |
| **Quantities / Properties Used** | _Molecular_ velocity, _Molecular_ energy                                      | Pressure, Temperature, Volume, Internal Energy                             |
| **Mathematical Requirement**     | Requires _statistical_ and _mathematical_ methods (large number of molecules) | Requires _simple mathematical formulae_                                    |
| **Observability**                | _Cannot be seen_ without powerful electron microscopes                        | _Can be sensed _  by human touch                                           |
| **Example**                      | _Kinetic theory of gases_                                                     | _Steam in a boiler_, gas in a cylinder                                     |
