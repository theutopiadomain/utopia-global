
> [!tip]  What is Calgon?
>  Calgon is a **water softening chemical** whose chemical name is **Sodium Hexametaphosphate** — (NaPO₃)₆


- **Calgon conditioning** is an **internal treatment method** used in boilers to prevent scale formation.
- it reacts with **hardness causing ions** like ==calcium== and ==magnesium== and converts them into **soluble complex compounds.**

### Principle
**Calgon combines** with ==Ca²⁺== and ==Mg²⁺== ions present in hard water and forms soluble complexes. Since the compounds formed are soluble, they remain dissolved in water and *do not form scale on boiler surfaces.*

**Reaction:**

$$2Ca²⁺ + (NaPO₃)₆ → [Ca₂(PO₃)₆]²⁻$$

---
> [!tip]   Why calgon is superior?

- **Prevents scale formation** — It converts hardness causing salts into soluble complexes, preventing scale deposition inside boiler.
- **Works at room temperature** — It works at room temperature, so no extra heating is required.
- **Removes existing scales** — It can also remove scales that have already formed inside the boiler.
- **Prevents corrosion** — It prevents corrosion by forming a thin protective film on the inner walls of the boiler.
- **Cost effective** — Only a very small amount of Calgon is needed, which makes it cost effective.
- **No pH change** — It does not change the pH of the water, water chemistry stays stable.
