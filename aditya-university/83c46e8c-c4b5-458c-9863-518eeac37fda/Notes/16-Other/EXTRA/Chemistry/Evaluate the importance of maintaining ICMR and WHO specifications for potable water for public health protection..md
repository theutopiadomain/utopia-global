> [!question] Why Are These Specifications Important?

**1. Prevention of Waterborne Diseases**

Unsafe water carries ==dangerous microorganisms== like *bacteria, viruses, and parasites*. ==Diseases== like *cholera, typhoid, and diarrhea* spread through contaminated water. 

**2. Protection from Harmful Chemicals**

Excess **fluoride** causes **fluorosis**, ==arsenic== causes ==skin cancer==, **nitrates** cause **blue baby syndrome**, and ==lead== causes ==brain damage== in children. These specifications fix safe upper limits for each chemical contaminant to prevent such serious health conditions.

**3. Ensuring Physical Quality**

**Standards** for color, odor, taste, and pH make sure ==water is not only safe but also acceptable to drink==. 

**4. Protecting Vulnerable Groups**

Infants, pregnant women, elderly people, and those with weak immunity are most at risk from contaminated water. By enforcing these standards, public health authorities provide extra protection to people who cannot defend themselves against even low levels of contamination.

**5. Reducing Disease Burden and Healthcare Costs**

Waterborne diseases put enormous pressure on hospitals and healthcare systems. Maintaining water quality standards reduces the number of preventable illnesses, saving money for both governments and families and allowing healthcare resources to be used more effectively.
