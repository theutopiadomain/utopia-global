### Reversible Process

A **reversible process** is an ideal thermodynamic process that can proceed **forward and backward without leaving any change** in the system or surroundings.

---
### Irreversible Process

An **irreversible process** is a real process that **cannot return the system and surroundings to their original state** without leaving changes.

---
In practice, **all natural processes are irreversible**, while reversible processes serve as **ideal models for thermodynamic analysis**.

---
### Causes of Irreversibility

Irreversibility occurs due to several natural effects:

1. **Friction**  
    Mechanical friction in pistons, turbines, pumps converts useful energy into heat.
    
2. **Heat transfer through finite temperature difference**  
    When heat flows from a hot body to a cooler body.
    
3. **Unrestrained (free) expansion of gases**  
    Gas expands suddenly into vacuum without doing work.
    
4. **Mixing of different substances**  
    Example: mixing two gases or liquids.
    
5. **Inelastic deformation**  
    Permanent deformation of solids.
