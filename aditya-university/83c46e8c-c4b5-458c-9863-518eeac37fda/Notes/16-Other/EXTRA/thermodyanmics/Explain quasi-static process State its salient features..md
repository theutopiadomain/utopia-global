Infinite slowness is a characteristic of the **Quasi (almost)** -**Static process** (or) it is a process by joining a series of equilibrium states

![[Pasted image 20260315211235.png]]

Consider a gas contained in a piston–cylinder arrangement.
• Initially, the system is in equilibrium at pressure P1, volume V1, and temperature T1. The
external load on the piston exactly balances the gas pressure.
• If the load on the piston is reduced very gradually in small increments, the system
remains nearly in equilibrium at every instant.
• The piston moves upward slowly, and the process passes through a continuous series of
equilibrium states. Such a process is called a quasi-static process.
