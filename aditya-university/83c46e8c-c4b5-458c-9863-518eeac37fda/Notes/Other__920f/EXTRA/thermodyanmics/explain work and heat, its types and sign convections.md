### Types of Heat

**1. Sensible Heat** Heat that causes a **change in temperature** of a substance, with no change in phase.

$Q=m⋅c⋅ΔT$

Where m = mass, c= specific heat, ΔT = temperature change.

_Example: Heating water from 30°C to 80°C._

---

**2. Latent Heat** Heat that causes a **change in phase** (solid ↔ liquid ↔ gas) with **no change in temperature**.

$$Q=m⋅L$$

Where L = latent heat of the substance.

_Example: Ice melting at 0°C — temperature stays constant while phase changes._
