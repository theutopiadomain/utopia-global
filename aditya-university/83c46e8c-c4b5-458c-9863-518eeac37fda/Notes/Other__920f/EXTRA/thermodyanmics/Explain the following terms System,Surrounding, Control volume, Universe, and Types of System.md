# System
*It is the part of the universe that we choose to study*  
(or)  
the quantity of fixed mass under investigation.
Example: Hot tea in a cup.

# Surrounding
*Everything external to the system is surroundings.*
Example: Air, table, room around the cup.

# Universe
*Everything, system and surroundings is called as universe*

---
### A system can be

1. [[Open system]] aka control volume → Both mass and energy can be transferred across the boundary.
    
2. [[Closed system]] aka control mass → Only energy (heat and work) can cross the boundary; mass remains constant.
    
3. [[Isolated system ]]→ Neither mass nor energy (in any form) can cross the boundary.
---
**And**

4. **Homogeneous** → Same everywhere — One phase (coffee + milk)(liquid + liquid)
    
5. **Heterogeneous** → Exists in different phases (water + ice) (liquid + solid)

If you drill down to the [[Microscopic and Macroscopic Viewpoints | microscopic]] view, the molecules are not homogeneous. But thermodynamics takes birds-eye-view.

---

**Note:**  
Most engineering systems are open systems.
